20. Two dice are thrown simultaneously. What is the probability that the sum of the numbers appearing on the dice is (\romannumeral1) 7? (\romannumeral2) a prime number? (\romannumeral3) 1?

\solution Two dice are thrown simultaneously. So, total number of possible outcomes = 36

(\romannumeral1) Sum of the numbers appearing on dice is 7
The favourable outcomes are (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), and (5,2).
Number of favourable outcomes = 6
\begin{align}
\text{Required probability} = \frac{6}{36} = \frac{1}{6}
\end{align}

(\romannumeral2) Sum of the numbers appearing on dice is a prime number.
The favourable outcomes are (1,1), (1,2), (1,4), (1,6), (2,1), (2,3), (2,5), (3,2), (3,4), (4,1), (4,3), (5,2), (5,6), (6,1) and (6,5).
Number of favourable outcomes = 15
\begin{align}
\text{Required probability} = \frac{15}{36} = \frac{5}{12}
\end{align}

(\romannumeral3) Sum of the numbers appearing on the dice is 1. It is not possible, so the probability is zero.
